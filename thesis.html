Investment thesis

We invest where the operating gap is visible.

Parabolic Capital looks for sectors where fragmented records, manual coordination, weak visibility, and under-built workflows create room for patient operating advantage.

Core view

Better systems can become better institutions.

The group is organized around practical sectors rather than fashion cycles. Each interest begins with a real operating need: bookings, ledgers, contracts, compliance, payments, field data, or customer coordination.

The thesis is not that every market needs more software. It is that many durable markets need clearer operating memory, stronger governance, better distribution, and tools that make trust easier to earn and maintain.

01

Build from the workflow

Start with what operators already do, then reduce friction around the most repeated, valuable, and trust-sensitive work.

02

Own sector depth

Cluster interests by market function so each asset benefits from shared learning without losing its own focus.

03

Compound patiently

Prefer businesses that become more useful as they gain history, integrations, local knowledge, and customer trust.

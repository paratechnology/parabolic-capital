Teddy City Hotels

Hotel operations and public web presence for a hospitality business with rooms, bookings, amenities, food service, revenue records, staff users, and notifications.

SectorHospitality operations
SurfacePublic website, admin workspace, API, mobile-ready stack

This product is smaller in scope than QuickProHost, but it shows the same pattern: turning a physical business into a usable operating system.

Website

What it is

Teddy City Hotels needs digital support for the practical work of running a hotel: room inventory, bookings, amenity activity, orders, revenue, staff access, and guest-facing presence.

What I built

  • Room and booking workflows for operational tracking.
  • Admin users and protected management areas.
  • Modules for snooker, swimming, kitchen orders, revenue, and notification flows.
  • A public website surface that gives the business a presentable customer entry point.

Technical surface

The product uses Angular, Firebase hosting/services, an Express-style backend surface, Capacitor-capable dependencies, and a monorepo layout for web, server, and shared work.

Product judgment

Hotel software has to respect the pace of front-desk work. The goal was to keep the interface direct: record the guest, update the room, capture the money, and leave a trail.

I can discuss how the hotel modules are separated, how user roles are handled, and how public presence connects to internal operations.
